Our Campaign: No New Fossil Fuel Infrastructure

Extinction Rebellion Boston’s main campaign since January 2022 is demanding “No New Fossil Infrastructure”: The first step to a livable future is to stop building the infrastructure that powers destruction, which is already obsolete.

Extinction Rebellion Boston demands that the Healey administration ban New Fossil Fuel Infrastructure by immediately taking the following steps:

  • Issue a public announcement that the Governor is committed to a No New Fossil Fuel Infrastructure policy for the Commonwealth, including opposing projects under construction or currently in the permitting process
  • Issue a public commitment that the Governor will only appoint anti-new fossil fuel infrastructure policymakers to the Energy Facilities Siting Board and Department of Public Utilities, the key independent boards that permit new energy infrastructure
  • Work with Speaker Mariano and Senate President Spilka to introduce legislation that would permanently ban new fossil fuel infrastructure in Massachusetts

Banning new fossil fuel infrastructure includes:

  • NO new fossil fuel power plants
  • NO new residential or commercial gas connections
  • NO new or expanded natural gas distribution pipelines, transmission pipelines, or compressor stations
  • NO new liquified natural gas production facilities, storage facilities, or terminals
  • NO new gas stations or other gasoline and diesel infrastructure
  • NO new airports or airfield expansions​​​​​​​

Stopping new fossil fuel infrastructure under development includes:

  • NEC’s Liquified Natural Gas Facility in Charlton
  • LNG expansion to Douglas
  • "Modernization" projects in Lowell and Worcester
  • "Reliability" projects in Western Mass. and Sharon-to-Brockton
  • The Hopkinton-Ashland Transfer Line
  • Meter stations in Longmeadow and Charlton
  • Springfield to Longmeadow natural gas pipeline
  • Enbridge’s Maple Project
  • Airport expansion projects throughout Massachusetts including
  • Hyannis Airport expansion in Cape Cod
  • Lawrence Municipal Airport in North Andover
  • Plymouth Municipal Airport expansion
  • Beverley Airport expansion
  • Norwood Airport expansion

XR Boston has planned and executed numerous non-violent civil disobedience demonstrations to demand No New Fossil Fuel Infrastructure, including:

2022

14 public Die-Ins

April 12: Ban Fossil Fuels banner drop

April 22: Freedom From Fossil Fuels Tour

To celebrate Earth Day, climate activists from Extinction Rebellion Boston and other Boston area climate and social justice organizations held a march from Copley Square, up Boylston, and ended at the State House. We stopped at the offices of financial, legal, and corporate companies that are both financing and profiting off the climate and ecological emergency.

May 27: Occupy Eversource CEO Joe Nolan’s front yard

Extinction Rebellion Boston (XR) occupied the front of Eversource CEO Joseph Nolan’s house. We used an art installation and our bodies to protest Eversource’s construction of new fossil fuel infrastructure and the company’s failure to rapidly transition to clean, renewable sources of energy. We stayed at Nolan’s house for a full day practicing regenerative activities like singing, dancing, and yoga. See the above video!

SEPTEMBER WEEK OF REBELLION INCLUDING

September 17: XR Youth’s Dinosaur Die-In

Rebels of all ages met at Faneuil Hall to "die-in" for climate change awareness. Participants dressed as dinosaurs to demonstrate humanity's imminent extinction, and the costumes did not disappoint!

September 20: Die In at Government Center

Rebels held a soulful public funeral for life on Planet Earth! After an initial Die-In and flyering in front of the MBTA station, attendees used nonviolent civil disobedience to block traffic on Tremont St. with a banner. As the "dead" lay in the street, Red Rebels processed slowly around them, the physical manifestations of climate grief.

September 21: Morning Disruption in Downtown Boston

XR Boston created a massive general disruption in morning rush-hour traffic throughout the financial district, eventually occupying the Seaport bridge. 15 arrests were made, and the action was reported on CBS (link includes news video), WCVB 5 , WHDH 7 , NBC 10 , Fox , Boston 25 , Boston Globe , Boston Herald , WBUR , Telemundo , and WGBH . We got a picture gallery in USA Today . Mayor Wu directly addressed the protest in an interview for WHDH 7 .

September 24: Stop Burning Down the House March

Rebels met at Dewey Square to practice chants and distribute art. Then, led by the live marching music of the Good Trouble Brass Band , Rebels marched up Atlantic Avenue to Christopher Columbus Waterfront Park, blocking 2-3 lanes of traffic with joyful/rageful chanting and flag-waving. Once gathered at the park, Rebels shared food while the Art Working Group set up a printmaking station.

December 15 - Atlantic Aviation Occupation

Members of Scientist Rebellion and Extinction Rebellion barricaded the driveway of Atlantic Aviation at MassPort’s Hanscom Field, protesting the continued use of private jets in the face of climate chaos. Rebels also entered the Atlantic Aviation offices and peacefully occupied their lobby. Ten Rebels were arrested, 6 women and 4 men total.

2023

Monthly public Die-Ins

Activists lie on the ground to represent the lives lost, human and non-human, from climate change. The imagery of the Die-Ins encourage conversation from passersby about the climate crisis.

January 5: Demanding No New Fossil Fuel Infrastructure at Governor Healey’s Inauguration

Activists stood in the inaugural crowd holding a large banner that read, “No New Fossil Fuel Infrastructure,” chanting and singing over the crowd. Afterwards we delivered a letter to newly inaugurated Governor Healey demanding No New Fossil Fuel Infrastructure.

February 9: Occupying Governor Healey’s office

More than 50 climate activists from Extinction Rebellion Boston (XR) held a sit-in at the State House. The non-violent activists blocked the governor's office and refused to leave. Disrupting movement in and out of key State House areas, activists risking arrest held signs and cloth banners reading "No New Fossil Fuel Infrastructure," "Gov. Healey: Climate Action Now," "No New Gas Hookups," "Stop Peabody Peaker Plant," and "Stop Springfield Gas Pipeline." 14 Rebels were arrested.

April 21: Earth Day Rally

On Earth Day, more than 300 people gathered outside Boston City Hall and marched to the Massachusetts State House to protest the Commonwealth’s slow action in addressing the climate and ecological emergency.

May 10: Legislative Session Occupation

11 activists from Extinction Rebellion Boston and Scientist Rebellion disrupted a joint session of House and Senate members in the House Chamber of the Massachusetts State House. Reading from a list of demands, chanting, and preventing the legislators from proceeding with their planned session, activists risking arrest held a cloth banner reading "No New Fossil Fuel Infrastructure." 9 climate activists were arrested.

June 5: Launch of the perpetual State House Stand-Out

Volunteers begin standing outside the Massachusetts State House every business day from 9 am to 5 pm, engaging passersby in conversations about climate change and pressuring lawmakers to ban new fossil fuel infrastructure projects.

June 15: XR Boston Moons Legislators

8 activists from Extinction Rebellion Boston (XR) mooned legislature with the message "STOP PASSING GAS" written on their backsides, demanding that the Massachusetts government immediately ban all new fossil fuel infrastructure and stop ongoing construction of new projects. The demonstration disrupted a formal Senate session. All eight Rebels were arrested.

SEPTEMBER WEEK OF REBELLION INCLUDING

September 18: State House Disruption - Stop playing games with our future!

More than 30 climate activists from Extinction Rebellion Boston (XR) played a creative version of basketball in the halls of the Massachusetts State House, to demand that Governor Maura Healey "stop playing games with our future." 9 Rebels were arrested.

September 21: Massive general disruption in Boston’s financial district

More than 35 climate activists from Extinction Rebellion Boston (XR) shut down traffic around the financial district to demand that the Healey administration ban new fossil fuel infrastructure. 20 activists were arrested after blocking rush hour traffic for almost 30 minutes. This demonstration was quickly covered by Boston Globe , Boston.com , Boston Herald , and NBC , then spread across national news through the Associated Press .

October 13: Logan Airport Expansion Protest

As more than 500 attendees gathered, including Mayor Michelle Wu and Governor Maura Healey, Extinction Rebellion Boston (XR), Airport Impact Relief Inc., Mothers Out Front, GreenRoots, and other members of the Logan Community Clean Air Coalition disrupted the Logan Airport's 100th anniversary celebration in the newly expanded International Terminal E.

2024

February 1: Disrupt Inundation District Screening

Activists from Extinction Rebellion Boston (XR) disrupted a moderated discussion with Climate Chief Melissa Hoffer, which took place after a screening of the documentary Inundation District .

April 20: Hanscom Airfield Disruption

Climate activists from Extinction Rebellion Boston (XR) disrupted business as usual at Laurence G. Hanscom Airfield to protest the proposed expansion of 17 new private jet hangars. 20 Rebels were arrested. Word of the action quickly spread across international news . Lex Media, a local news source, created a short documentary about the demonstration. Inspired by this public outcry, celebrity activist Ashley Judd even took up the cause, speaking publicly against the Hanscom expansion. MassPort responded by extending the public comment period on the expansion for a full month, allowing for more community input.

April 22: Earth Day Parade

After a family-friendly community party at the Christopher Columbus Waterfront Park, Extinction Rebellion Boston and affiliated groups marched along downtown Boston's Greenway and occupied the Seaport Boulevard bridge in a mass action marking Earth Day.
